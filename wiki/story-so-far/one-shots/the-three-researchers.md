# One-Shot: The Three Researchers

Played on: 22/11/25
Characters:
- Arma (played by Buzz)
- Artillery (played by Haikal)
- Ampere (played by Toph)
- Zel'eon (played by Haikal)
- Dregdul (played by Rafiq)
- Herald of Pitchus (played by Rafiq)

## Preamble

6 months after the Seraphs broke the extraplanar barrier, three of the foremost researchers in D:IX have detected their biggest incursion yet. EchoTech isn't happy about this, since the incursion is set to occur in Sector 5 where the Rath Zidul insurgents have been making moves after taking Sector 6 a few months ago. Regardles, they manage to convince their bosses to let them research it, with the promise that they will get out of there immediately if Zel'eon shows up.

Oh yeah - these researchers are also so hard set on this because they want to find a cure to their mutations, a result of the incursions, that have left them with powers, but with monstrous physical mutations as well.


## SCENE 1: Entering and Breaking

It's midday on AA 0.6.12, and Arma, Artillery, and Ampere are in an armoured EchoTech transport on their way to the site of the potential incursion in Sector 5. They discuss the risk of the rebels showing up, but resolve to just get in and out as fast as possible, knowing that studying an incursion of this size could lead to a breakthrough in their research. Their handler, DS Carter, stops the van once they're at the location of the incursion and tell them to wait until they secure the perimeter, to make sure no citizens see them.

Once the coast is clear, they rush to set up their equipment. As Ampere sets up a shield generator, Artillery looks through a pair of goggles they have tuned to the resonance of the rifts. To his horror, he sees two gloved demonic hands, one in red and one in white and each larger than the width of the road below it, appearing to be the one tearing it open. Warning the rest, they set up all their backup shield generators, creating five overlapping hard light spheres around the rift.

Just as they finish setting up their equipment, they see mottled maroon skin in the rift's maw. A huge, corpulent demon emerges belly-first out of the rift, naming itself as the herald of Pithcus, the Lord of Despair. Trapped in the spheres, it begins attempting to break out while the researchers do their best to trick it and strengthen the shields to force it back through the rift before it closes. DS Carter attempts to call for reinforcements at this point, but receives no communication from his troops in the area.

As the researchers begin to finally force the herald demon back into the rift, they hear a sharp whistling sound. Zel'eon Talos, leader of the Rath Zidul insurgency, swoops down from the sky and slices through the shield generators. Almost paralyzed by fear at the Dragonslayer showing up, the researchers attempt to flee to their armoured transport, but are pinned down by gunfire by insurgents who come out of the alleyways.

As the herald demon escapes the rift, Zel'eon flies up to meet its gaze, and it grins, recognising him as the Dragonslayer. It grabs at him, but he evades easily.Groaning in hunger, its arm suddenly shoots out, stretching up to the highway and grabbing a car, crushing it and killing almost all of the family that was inside, leaving only a little elf boy in his grasp. The maw of the herald demon opens, and it begins lowering the boy into it, as he cries and shouts in fear.

As this happens, the researchers finally make it into the van and are about to get in when they see this happening. In a desperate attempt to save the boy, Artillery spits a glob of acid directly at the herald demon's face before diving in as Ampere closes the door behind him. As the herald demon grabs its face, screaming in pain, Zel'eon wings down and grabs the falling boy, depositing him by one of his men. Seeing the extremely potent effect Artillery's acid had on the demon, the insurgent leader gets an idea.

As the three researchers cower in fear, waiting for their inevitable end, an unfamiliar voice comes in on their private comms. Zel'eon, standing outside the van, simply asks them, "Hey, Echofreaks. Wanna make a deal?".

## Scene 2: A Demon, Dragon, and Three Mutants Walk into A Bar

As the herald demon stomps around in pain, screaming still from the acid on its face, Zel'eon convinces the researchers to come out of the van and join him in fighting the demon, knowing he can't do it alone. He promises them freedom and that he will not hurt them, and they reluctantly believe him, thinking they have no other choice.

The insurgents and the three researchers prepare for battle as the herald demon finally gets the last of the acid off its face, now angry and ready to wreak havoc. In the melee that ensues, Zel'eon commands his men well, using the researchers as close range combatants and adjusting the aim of his snipers manually to throw the herald demon off, ensuring it never gets a single hit in while the rest wear him down.

Eventually, a well placed spike of keratin by Arma pierces the herald demon's skull, and a barrage of ranged attacks leads to its head being partially blown off, killing it for good. Though knowing what the answer will be, Arma still turns to Zel'eon and asks if they are really free to go. The insurgent leader grins, telling them that he said **he** wouldn't hurt them. As the three researchers are cuffed and stuffed into a van, they hear the sounds of Zel'eon and the insurgents using the corpse of the herald demon as a photo op.

They stop eventually, and get out at what seems to be the insurgent base of operations, a retrofitted auto shop owned by one Dregdul, the second-in-command of the insurgency. As they are welcomed in, Zel'eon arrives, and Arma and Ampere beg to be allowed to continue their research under Rath Zidul, professing no loyalty to EchoTech. Zel'eon directs his men to take them away, before turning to Artillery who had been silent and looking down at the table the whole time.

Unbeknownst to anyone, Artillery had been drawing mana into himself the whole time. As soon as the other two researchers were taken away, he looks at Zel'eon with fury in his eyes. He opens his mouth, intending to blow himself up taking Zel'eon and Dregdul with him, but wanting to say his piece first.

He never got the chance.

The Dragonslayer's arm-blade punched through his chest as a blur. As the last rasps of his voice eked out, Zel'eon kicks him off of his blade, and as Arthur O'Leary falls to the ground, blue wisps of mana float out of his punctured lung. As everything went black, his final thoughts escaped his mouth - "I'm sorry... EchoTech...".
